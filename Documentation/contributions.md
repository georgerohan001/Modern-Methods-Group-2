# 1.2 Author contributions

The project involved repeated phases of annotation and model training, followed by a final training stage using the four-channel dataset and evaluation on independent test data. All four group members initially contributed to dataset creation by selecting two to three trees each, generating two-dimensional slices, and annotating them in CVAT. Model training was conducted on a single workstation and was therefore executed by **George**, while the project folder was shared via Google Drive so that all group members could review results and suggest improvements.

**Beatrice** and **Stephen** expanded the training dataset by refining and extending annotations based on preliminary model predictions. In parallel, **George** and **Jonathan** worked on adapting the model to accept the four-channel input representation required for the final training.

For evaluation, each of us annotated 20 slices from trees originating from a different study area and species composition. **Jonathan** then used this dataset to evaluate the trained model and generate the performance metrics. The results were then jointly reviewed and interpreted by the entire group.

Finally, **George** and **Jonathan** drafted the written report, while Beatrice and Stephen focused on reviewing, editing, and polishing the final text.
